Top Right Triangle

Definition:

Top Right Triangle is a Yard chart pattern comparable to an imaginary right-angled triangle, it is made up two sides at a 90 degree angle to each other, the are linked by a hypotenuse. In the particular field of financial technical analysis we make the following associations:

Picture A

  1. Long side: is the horizontal line drawn by uniting two or more approximately equal market lows in a given temporal segment . See line 1, in picture A
  2. Short side: is the imaginary vertical line whith length equal to the distance from the the Horizontal line and the market high formed above the horizontal. See line 2, in picture A
  3. Hypotenuse : is the imaginary sloping line that results by linking the market highs corresponding to the bounces formed above the horizontal. See line 3, in picture A

The ideal model of Top Right Triangle has the following characteristics:

- The bounces above the horizontal line must be at least 2, preferably three, and each corresponding market high starting from left must be in descending order.

- Any of these bounces are on average preferable when the velocity from the horizontal to the market high is less than the down velocity from the high back to the horizontal. This will result in the up bars from the horizontal to the high being more/equal in number than the down bars. Not all bounces in a triangle will always look like this but it is important that the last bounce before the break out presents this characteristic. On average these will be better triangles than the rest.

Picture B


- The formation of a Top Right Triangle is confirmed when the market breaks and closes below the horizontal line, having previously formed at least two bounces. At this point we have a sell signal, and a stop loss must be placed just above the high of the last bounce before the break out. See picture B that depicts all these properties.
