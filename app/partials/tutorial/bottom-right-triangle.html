Bottom Right Triangle

Definition:

Bottom Right Triangle is a Yard chart pattern comparable to an imaginary right-angled triangle, it is made up two sides at a 90 degree angle to each other, the are linked by a hypotenuse. In the particular field of financial technical analysis we make the following associations:

Picture A

  1. LONG SIDE: is the horizontal line drawn by uniting two or more approximately equal market highs in a given temporal segment . See line 1, in picture A
  2. SHORT SIDE: is the imaginary vertical line whose high is equal to the distance from the the Horizontal line and the market low formed beneath the horizontal. See line 2, in picture A
  3. HYPOTENUSE: is the imaginary sloping line that results by linking the market lows corresponding to the bounces formed under the horizontal. See line 3, in picture A

The ideal model of Bottom Right Triangle has the following characteristics:

- The bounces under the horizontal line must be at least 2, preferably three, and each corresponding market low starting from left must be in ascending order.

- Any of these bounces are on average preferable when the velocity from the horizontal to the market low is less than the up velocity from the low back to the horizontal. This will result in the down bars from the horizontal to the low being more/equal in number than the up bars. Not all bounces in a triangle will always be like this but it is important that the last bounce before the break out presents this characteristic. On average these will be better triangles than the rest.

Picture B


- The formation of a Bottom Right Triangle is confirmed when the market breaks and closes above the horizontal line, having previously formed at least two bounces. At this point we have a buy signal, and a stop loss must be placed just below the low of the last bounce before the break out. See picture B that depicts all these properties.
